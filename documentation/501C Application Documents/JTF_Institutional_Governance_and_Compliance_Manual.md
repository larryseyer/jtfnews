# Just the Facts News

# Institutional Governance & Compliance Manual

*Last Updated: 2026-02-14*

# SECTION I --- ORGANIZATIONAL OVERVIEW

## 1. Mission

Just the Facts News (JTF News) is organized exclusively for charitable
and educational purposes under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue
Code.

The organization develops and distributes a structured, transparent
methodology for factual reporting without commentary, opinion, or
advocacy. Its purpose is to provide the public with accessible summaries
of verifiable recent events using defined verification and sourcing
standards.

We record events.\
We do not interpret them.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# SECTION II --- IRS FORM 1023 NARRATIVE (ACTIVITIES)

## 2. Primary Activities

### A. Structured Factual Reporting

JTF News operates a continuously updated global news stream. Each
published item:

-   Meets predefined inclusion thresholds for public significance\
-   Is corroborated by at least two unrelated qualifying sources\
-   Is processed through rule-based language transformation to remove
    evaluative or speculative language\
-   Includes source ownership transparency where publicly available

No opinion, commentary, endorsements, or calls to action are published.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### B. Methodology Publication

The organization publishes:

-   Public documentation of verification standards\
-   Ownership disclosure methodology\
-   Correction logs\
-   Version-controlled updates

All methodology materials are publicly accessible for educational
purposes.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### C. Community Replication

JTF News provides open licensing (CC-BY-SA) for communities to adopt the
methodology for local informational channels.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. Political Activity Compliance

JTF News does not:

-   Participate in or intervene in political campaigns\
-   Endorse or oppose candidates\
-   Rate political actors\
-   Engage in lobbying or advocacy

Government actions may be reported strictly as structured factual
events.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 4. Revenue Sources

Revenue is derived from:

-   Individual donations\
-   Foundation grants\
-   Optional early-access technical memberships

No advertising revenue is accepted. No donor may influence editorial
methodology or content selection.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 5. Public Benefit

The organization provides educational benefit by:

-   Increasing transparency in sourcing\
-   Providing structured factual summaries\
-   Maintaining open documentation\
-   Supporting informed civic awareness without persuasion

# SECTION IV --- CORE POLICIES

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 7. Conflict of Interest Policy

### Purpose

To protect organizational integrity and ensure compliance with Section
501(c)(3).

### Definition

A conflict exists when a director, officer, or key employee has a
financial or governance interest in: - A media outlet covered by JTF
News\
- A vendor serving JTF News\
- Any entity that could materially benefit from decisions

### Disclosure

-   Annual written disclosures required\
-   Updates required upon material change\
-   Mandatory recusal from related votes

### Board Review

Conflicts and recusals are documented in meeting minutes.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 8. Donor Non-Interference Policy

Financial contributions shall not influence editorial methodology,
verification standards, inclusion thresholds, or publication timing.

Donors may not: - Request specific coverage\
- Influence source selection\
- Influence correction decisions\
- Influence publication order

Major donor disclosures will comply with applicable law.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 9. Editorial Firewall Policy

### Structural Separation

Fundraising, operations, and editorial methodology functions remain
separate.

### Authority

Methodology is governed by documented rules and version control.

Changes require: - Written proposal\
- Public documentation\
- Board acknowledgment

### External Direction Prohibited

No donor, sponsor, board member, or staff member may override
verification thresholds or publication rules.

### Audit

Periodic internal audits verify adherence.

# SECTION V --- COMPLIANCE & RISK MANAGEMENT

## 10. Political Activity Safeguards

To maintain 501(c)(3) compliance:

-   No candidate endorsements
-   No campaign intervention
-   No lobbying activity
-   No comparative political ratings
-   No calls to action

All coverage of government actors is factual and non-evaluative.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 11. Corrections & Transparency

-   Corrections issued promptly
-   Archives amended and marked
-   Public corrections log maintained
-   Version-controlled methodology updates

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 12. Data & Records

-   Structured summaries archived publicly
-   Raw data retained no longer than operationally necessary
-   Governance records maintained according to nonprofit best practices

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# SECTION VI --- DISSOLUTION

Upon dissolution, all assets shall be distributed exclusively for
charitable or educational purposes consistent with Section 501(c)(3).
