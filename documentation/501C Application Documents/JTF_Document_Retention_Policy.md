# Just the Facts News

## Document Retention and Destruction Policy

*Last Updated: 2026-02-14*

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes standards for the retention and timely destruction of documents and records of Just the Facts News (JTF News). It is adopted to ensure compliance with applicable federal and state law, to satisfy the governance expectations of the Internal Revenue Service for Section 501(c)(3) organizations, and to protect the organization's interests and the privacy of individuals associated with the organization.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all documents and records, whether in physical or electronic form, created, received, or maintained by JTF News in connection with its operations. It applies to all directors, officers, employees, contractors, and volunteers.

---

## 3. Definitions

**Document** means any written, printed, or electronic material, including but not limited to paper records, electronic files, emails, databases, spreadsheets, presentations, audio and video recordings, and any other medium that contains information.

**Destruction** means the physical shredding of paper documents or the permanent, irrecoverable deletion of electronic records.

---

## 4. Retention Schedule

### 4.1 Corporate and Governance Records

Articles of incorporation, bylaws, and amendments thereto shall be retained permanently. Board meeting minutes and resolutions shall be retained permanently. Annual reports and filings with state agencies shall be retained permanently. Conflict of interest disclosures shall be retained for seven years after the individual's separation from the organization. Policies adopted by the Board shall be retained permanently, with prior versions retained for seven years after supersession.

### 4.2 Tax and Financial Records

IRS Form 1023 application and determination letter shall be retained permanently. IRS Forms 990 and all supporting schedules shall be retained permanently. General ledger and journals shall be retained for seven years. Accounts payable and receivable records shall be retained for seven years. Bank statements and reconciliations shall be retained for seven years. Audit reports shall be retained permanently. Investment records shall be retained for seven years after disposition.

### 4.3 Employment and Personnel Records

Employee personnel files shall be retained for seven years after separation. Payroll records and tax filings (W-2, 1099, etc.) shall be retained for seven years. Benefit plan records shall be retained for seven years after plan termination. Workers' compensation records shall be retained for ten years. I-9 forms shall be retained for three years after hire or one year after termination, whichever is later.

### 4.4 Contracts and Legal Records

Executed contracts shall be retained for seven years after expiration or termination. Leases shall be retained for seven years after expiration. Insurance policies and claims records shall be retained permanently. Legal correspondence and litigation files shall be retained permanently or as directed by legal counsel. Intellectual property registrations and licenses shall be retained permanently.

### 4.5 Donor and Grant Records

Donor records and gift acknowledgments shall be retained for seven years. Grant agreements, reports, and correspondence shall be retained for seven years after the grant period ends. Fundraising records shall be retained for seven years.

### 4.6 Editorial and Methodology Records

Published content and associated source documentation shall be retained permanently. Methodology documentation (all versions) shall be retained permanently. Correction logs shall be retained permanently. Editorial audit records shall be retained for seven years.

### 4.7 Electronic Communications

Email and electronic messages related to any of the above categories shall be retained for the period applicable to the relevant category. Routine administrative communications not related to any retained category may be destroyed after one year.

---

## 5. Destruction Procedures

### 5.1 Authorized Destruction

Documents that have exceeded their required retention period may be destroyed in accordance with this policy. Destruction shall be conducted or supervised by the Executive Director or a designee.

### 5.2 Method of Destruction

Physical documents containing confidential or sensitive information shall be shredded using cross-cut shredding equipment. Electronic records shall be permanently deleted using methods that prevent recovery. The Executive Director shall maintain a destruction log recording the date of destruction, a general description of materials destroyed, and the name of the individual who conducted or authorized the destruction.

### 5.3 Exceptions and Litigation Hold

No documents shall be destroyed if they are relevant to any pending or reasonably anticipated litigation, government investigation, or audit. Upon becoming aware of any such matter, the Executive Director shall issue a written litigation hold notice to all affected individuals, suspending the normal destruction schedule for all potentially relevant documents. The litigation hold shall remain in effect until released in writing by the Executive Director with the advice of legal counsel.

---

## 6. Compliance and Enforcement

### 6.1 Responsibility

The Executive Director is responsible for the administration of this policy. The Audit & Compliance Committee of the Board of Directors shall review compliance with this policy on an annual basis.

### 6.2 Violations

Failure to comply with this policy, including the unauthorized destruction of documents or the failure to observe a litigation hold, may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination, and may expose the individual and the organization to legal liability.

---

## 7. Electronic Records and Backups

The organization shall maintain regular electronic backups of all digital records. Backup media shall be stored securely and shall be subject to the same retention periods as the underlying records. When electronic records are scheduled for destruction, the organization shall ensure that all copies, including backup copies, are permanently destroyed.

---

## 8. Amendment

This policy may be amended by majority vote of the Board of Directors. Amendments shall be documented and communicated to all covered individuals.
